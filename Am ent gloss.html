<h1>Invertebrate body structure</h1>

<p>
A group of terms all related to the various parts of an invertebrate's body.</p>

<h2>Terms in this group</h2>

<ul>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/abdomen">Abdomen</a><br />one of the three main body parts of an insect and one of the two main body parts of spiders.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/acrostichal-bristles">Acrostichal Bristles</a><br />bristles found on the top of the thorax of some flies.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/aedeagus">Aedeagus</a><br />the reproductive organ of male insects.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/androconia">Androconia</a><br />scales on the wings of male butterflies and moths which produce scent.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/antenna">Antenna</a><br />paired sensory organs on the head of insects.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/arista">Arista</a><br />a bristle found on the antennae of some species of fly.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/arthropod">Arthropod</a><br />invertebrate animals that have a jointed exoskeleton.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/asynchronous-muscle">Asynchronous muscle</a><br />muscle that contracts more than once per nerve impulse.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/axilla">Axilla</a><br />the point at which thoracic muscles attach to the wing of an insect.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/book-lung">Book lung</a><br />the main respiratory organ in many arachnids.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/brachypterous">Brachypterous</a><br />a term used to describe an animal with short or reduced wings.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/calypter">Calypter</a><br />a membranous flap that is located at the base of the wing in some species of fly.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/cephalothorax">Cephalothorax</a><br />the anterior most of the two body sections of an arachnid.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/cerci">Cerci</a><br />paired appendages on the abdomen of many insects.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/chelae">Chelae</a><br />pincers or claws at the end of arachnid or crustacean appendages.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/chitin">Chitin</a><br />a tough, translucent material found in arthropod exoskeletons.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/claw">Claw</a><br />the final segment in the leg of an insect.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/clypeus">Clypeus</a><br />one of the plates forming the 'face' of an insect.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/compound-eye">Compound eye</a><br />the main visual organ in insects.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/cornicle">Cornicle</a><br />tubular projections from the abdomen of an aphid.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/costa">Costa</a><br />a region or vein at the front of an insect's wing.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/coxa">Coxa</a><br />the first segment (closest to the body) of an insect leg.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/crop">Crop</a><br />part of an insect's gut. The crop is often used as a temporary store for food.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/cuneus">Cuneus</a><br />a triangular region found on the fore wings of some Hemiptera.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/cursorial">Cursorial</a><br />a phrase used to describe the legs of some insects and other invertebrates that are modified for running.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/cuticle">Cuticle</a><br />part of the external skeleton of arthropods.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/direct-flight-muscles">Direct flight muscles</a><br />direct flight muscles are muscles connected to the wing of an insect.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/discal-cell">Discal cell</a><br />a cell in the wing of some insects.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/distal">Distal</a><br />the part of an appendage (leg, wing, antenna etc) that is farthest from the body.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/dorsal">Dorsal</a><br />the upper surface of an organism.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/ecdysis">Ecdysis</a><br />moulting of the arthropod cuticle.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/elytra">Elytra</a><br />the hardened fore wings of beetles and earwigs.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/empodium">Empodium</a><br />A lobe or spine between the tarsal claws of many insects.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/epiproct">Epiproct</a><br />a projection from an insect's tenth abdominal segment.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/exoskeleton">Exoskeleton</a><br />a skeleton that is on the outside and encases all the muscles and organs of an organism.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/eyespot">Eyespot</a><br />markings on an insect, usually the wings, that have a similar appearance to a mammalian eye. Commonly used to scare off potential predators.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/facet">Facet</a><br />the hexagonal face on the ommatidia within a compound eye.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/fang">Fang</a><br />hollow, pointed structures attached to the chelicerae of spiders that are used to deliver venom. Centipedes also possess fangs but these are different in structure.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/femur">Femur</a><br />part of the insect leg between trochanter and tibia.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/filiform">Filiform</a><br />a term used to describe thin, thread-like antennae.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/fossorial">Fossorial</a><br />a phrase used to describe the legs of some insects and other invertebrates that are modified for digging.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/fovea">Fovea</a><br />an area in the compound eye that provides greater visual acuity than the rest of the eye.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/frenulum">Frenulum</a><br />a row of bristles used to connect the fore and hind wings in some insects.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/gaster">Gaster</a><br />the swollen section of a Hymenopteran abdomen.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/glossa">Glossa</a><br />an insect's tongue.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/gut">Gut</a><br />the main digestive organ in insects.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/haemocoel">Haemocoel</a><br />the blood filled body cavity of many invertebrates.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/haemolymph">Haemolymph</a><br />insect blood.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/haltere">Haltere</a><br />a modified wing that is reduced to form a 'drum stick' shaped balance organ.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/head">Head</a><br />the anterior most of the three main body parts of an insect.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/hydrostatic-skeleton">Hydrostatic skeleton</a><br />a type of exoskeleton supported by fluid pressure.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/indirect-flight-muscles">Indirect flight muscles</a><br />indirect flight muscles do not attach directly to the wing of an insect. Flapping is accomplished by deforming the shape of the thorax.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/integument">Integument</a><br />the outer covering of an insect's body.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/johnstons-organ">Johnstons organ</a><br />a sensory organ used to detect sound near the base of the antenna.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/jugum">Jugum</a><br />an area of the fore wing in some insects that overlaps the hind wing and holds them together.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/juvenile-hormone">Juvenile hormone</a><br />a hormone that inhibits development of a juvenile insect to an adult.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/labium">Labium</a><br />an insect's lower lip.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/labrum">Labrum</a><br />an insect's upper lip.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/lateral">Lateral</a><br />the side of an organism.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/malpighian-tubules">Malpighian tubules</a><br />the main excretory system of insects and some other invertebrates.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/mandibles">Mandibles</a><br />a pair of mouthparts used for biting or cutting in Arthropods.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/maxilla">Maxilla</a><br />part of an insect's mouthparts. They are paired and used to hold or manipulate food.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/ocelli">Ocelli</a><br />ocelli are simple light detecting organs.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/ocularium">Ocularium</a><br />a raised structure on the body of an Opilione that contains the eyes.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/ommatidia">Ommatidia</a><br />the individual unit within an insect's compound eye.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/ovary">Ovary</a><br />female reproductive organ that produces eggs.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/ovipositor">Ovipositor</a><br />a tube that is used for laying eggs.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/pedicel">Pedicel</a><br />the thin structure connecting the thorax and abdomen.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/pedipalps">Pedipalps</a><br />appendages attached to the cephalothorax of arachnids.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/plastron">Plastron</a><br />an array of hairs or bumps used to trap air next to the body of some aquatic insects where it forms a physical gill.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/pollen-basket">Pollen basket</a><br />a structure on the legs of bees in which pollen is carried.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/proboscis">Proboscis</a><br />an elongated feeding tube attached to the head of an animal.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/pronotum">Pronotum</a><br />a plate-like cover of the first thoracic segment.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/pterostigma">Pterostigma</a><br />a dark pigmented spot on the leading edge and towards the tip of the wing in some species of insect.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/pulvilli">Pulvilli</a><br />Lobes or pads between the tarsal claws of many insects that help the insect stick to a surface. </li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/quadrilateral">Quadrilateral</a><br />a cell in the wing of a damselfly.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/radius">Radius</a><br />an important vein within the fore wings of insects.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/raptorial">Raptorial</a><br />a phrase used to describe the grasping fore legs of some insects and other invertebrates.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/reflex-bleeding">Reflex bleeding</a><br />a defensive behaviour in some insects where a noxious fluid is exuded by the insect when threatened.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/rostrum">Rostrum</a><br />a snout-like projection from the heads of some insects.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/saltatorial">Saltatorial</a><br />a phrase used to describe the legs of some insects and other invertebrates that are modified for jumping.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/spinneret">Spinneret</a><br />an organ that produces silk. Spinnerets are possessed by spiders and some species of insect.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/spiracle">Spiracle</a><br />openings on the thorax and abdomen of insects that allow the insect to breathe.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/sting">Sting</a><br />a sharp organ used by some invertebrates to deliver venom.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/stridulation">Stridulation</a><br />the act of producing sound, usually by rubbing two body parts together.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/subesophageal-ganglion">Subesophageal ganglion</a><br />a mass of nerve cells that control the mouthparts of insects.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/synchronous-muscle">Synchronous muscle</a><br />a type of muscle that beats once per nerve impulse.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/tagma">Tagma</a><br />the term used to describe each of the three main divisions of an insect's body.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/tarsomere">Tarsomere</a><br />sections within the tarsus - the final segment in the leg of an insect.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/tarsus">Tarsus</a><br />the final segment in the leg of an insect.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/tegmina">Tegmina</a><br />the thickened fore wing of some insects.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/thorax">Thorax</a><br />one of the three main body parts of an insect.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/tibia">Tibia</a><br />the fourth segment of an insect's leg.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/tomentum">Tomentum</a><br />fine hairs that cover the bodies of some insects.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/trachea">Trachea</a><br />tubes attached to the spiracles allowing oxygen to diffuse into an insect's body.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/tracheoles">Tracheoles</a><br />thin tubes attached to the trachea within the body of an insect and permit gaseous exchange.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/trichobothrium">Trichobothrium</a><br />hair-like structures of arachnids and some insects that are used to detect vibration.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/trochanter">Trochanter</a><br />the second segment of an insect's leg.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/tymbal">Tymbal</a><br />a cicada's sound producing organ.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/tympanal-organ">Tympanal organ</a><br />the hearing system of insects.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/uric-acid">Uric acid</a><br />a waste substance excreted by insects and many other terrestrial animals.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/urticating-hairs">Urticating hairs</a><br />defensive hairs possessed by some arachnids and insects that cause itching and irritation in mammals.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/vein">Vein</a><br />structures that support an insect's wing.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/venation">Venation</a><br />the arrangement of veins in an insect's wing. Venation is very important in the identification of insect species.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/ventral">Ventral</a><br />the lower surface of an organism.</li>
<li><a href="/insects/glossary/terms/wing-bud">Wing bud</a><br />flattened structures possessed by nymphs from which the wings will develop in the adult insect.</li>
</ul>
