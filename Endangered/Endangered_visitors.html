Wenatchee Mountains checkermallow: the seed predator question

The flower visitors pictured above are part of the beneficial insect community that supports a first step in plant reproduction: pollination and seed set. However for Sidalcea, most of the crop of large and abundant seed is consumed by weevil seed predators. This is well known among conservation groups that typically see >50% mortality of seeds for several Sidalcea species. In the case of S. oregana, the mortality has ranged from 75-99%. Clearly seed predation is an important aspect of the ecology and conservation of S. oregana var. calva (and perhaps others among the ~25 species in the western US). We are learning about the weevils - at least 3 species attack Sidalcea - and also the parasitoids that may play a role in suppressing weevil populations.
