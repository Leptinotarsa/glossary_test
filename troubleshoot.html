A a-z

abscissa

Not a specific vein, but a description of a segment of a wing vein that is delimited by the intersection of other veins. E.g., if Main Street were a vein, one abscissa would be the stretch between 1st and 2nd.

abdomen

For insects in general, the third, posterior body region. For bees, wasps, and ants, there is a wrinkle: the apparent abdomen is properly called the metasoma, because the first abdominal segment is actually integral to the second body region, the mesosoma, or apparent thorax. Taxonomic literature typically uses the technical term: metasoma. DiscoverLife prefers the less precise but more commonly understood term: abdomen.

acute

Sharply angled, > 90 degrees
